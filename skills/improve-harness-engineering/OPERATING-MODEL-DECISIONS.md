# Operating Model Decisions

Do not treat every friction point as accidental. Some friction is an intentional constraint that preserves runtime parity, quality, security, cost control, or human ownership.

If a reasonable team could choose the opposite recommendation based on product, runtime, workflow, cost, risk, or quality priorities, frame the recommendation as a decision instead of an implementation task.

Decision recommendations must include:

- decision needed
- options
- tradeoffs
- evidence needed
- what would settle the decision

Do not present decision-sensitive recommendations as implementation-ready. Preserve uncertainty explicitly.
