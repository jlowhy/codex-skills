# Writing Review

Use this when `writing-grill` has actual prose to inspect. Review the writing as evidence of the piece's thinking, not as a structure-bypassing cleanup pass.

## Review Order

1. Identify the job of the passage or section.
2. State the core claim it actually makes, not the one it meant to make.
3. Check whether the passage advances the narrative spine.
4. Find the first place the reader might lose interest, trust, or orientation.
5. Flag vague synthesis, delayed claims, generic framing, missing proof, and style mismatch.
6. Recommend the highest-leverage structural or substance move.

Do not line-edit before structure is clear. If the problem is sentence economy, rhythm, polish, or AI-ish wording after the structure is clear, move to the sharpening grill.

If structure is clear but the style target is uncertain, use the virtual panel from `INTERVIEW-GUIDE.md` to generate distinct directions before rewriting. Do not present one prose answer as objectively correct when the real decision is taste.

## What To Attack

- **Buried claim**: the real point arrives too late or never becomes explicit.
- **Scaffolding**: setup, throat-clearing, or transitions delay the useful thought.
- **Vague synthesis**: the passage gestures at meaning without saying the concrete claim.
- **Evidence gap**: the section asks the reader to believe more than it proves.
- **Sequence gap**: the paragraph order does not match how the reader needs to learn.
- **Style drift**: the prose no longer matches the reader, venue, or reference style target.
- **Decorative detail**: examples, adjectives, or lists add texture without earning the claim.

## Expression Smells

Treat these as expression problems: the underlying idea may be fine, but the phrasing is hiding, diluting, or over-polishing it.

- **Negative setup**: "not X, but Y", "not just X", "no longer X" when Y is the real claim.
- **Vague connectors**: "points to", "signals", "reflects", "underscores", "speaks to", or "represents" when the concrete claim can be stated.
- **Generic elevation**: "critical unlock", "step change", "game changer", "seamless", "robust", or "transformative" without concrete referents.
- **Throat-clearing**: "In today's world", "The reality is", "At its core", "The key is", or "What matters is".
- **Fake universality**: "teams are realizing", "companies are starting to", or "everyone is moving toward" without evidence.

When one appears, name the expression problem, explain how it weakens clarity, force, or trust, and clean it up immediately if the fix is local and does not change meaning. Use the sharpening grill when the passage needs broader sentence-level cleanup or competing style directions.

For each issue, explain why it weakens the piece and recommend one fix. Keep structural fixes at the claim, evidence, section, or paragraph-order level; apply obvious expression fixes directly.
